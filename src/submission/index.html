Submission Guidelines

We invite submissions across writing, visual work, and experimental forms that engage with questions of caste, gender, knowledge, and everyday life.

The platform is conceived as a space for critical yet accessible work, writing that is conceptually grounded without being restricted to specialist audiences. We welcome original contributions that have not been published or accepted for publication elsewhere.

General Style and Approach

All submissions should follow the Author–Date system of the Chicago Manual of Style. Citations must be included within the text, along with a reference list at the end. Hyperlinks may be used where appropriate, particularly for reports, digital archives, and online material.

We encourage clarity of expression. Submissions should avoid unnecessary jargon while retaining conceptual precision. Writing should be attentive not only to what is being argued, but also to how the argument is being constructed and situated.

Formats and Word Ranges

We accept submissions across a range of formats. The following word limits are indicative:

  • Short reflections / conceptual essays: 800–1,000 words
  • Opinion pieces (argument-driven): 1,000–1,500 words
  • Analytical essays / explainers: 1,500–2,500 words
  • Long-form research essays: 3,000–5,000 words
  • Book reviews: approximately 1,200 words
  • Keyword Digest entries: 800–1,200 words
  • Nazar (photo essays): up to 3 images with captions and a short note

We are less concerned with rigid categorization and more with the coherence, clarity, and intent of the submission.

Section-Specific Guidelines

Book Reviews

Book reviews should engage critically with the text rather than simply summarising it. Submissions should situate the book within its broader intellectual context, engage with its arguments and structure, and reflect on its contributions and limitations. Reviews should maintain a clear distinction between the author's argument and the reviewer's voice.

Explainers

Explainers should introduce a concept, issue, or debate in a way that is accessible to non-specialist readers while retaining analytical depth. Submissions should situate the topic within an ongoing conversation and present different perspectives where relevant.

Commentaries

Commentaries must take a clear and reasoned position on a question or contemporary issue. Arguments should be grounded in research, reading, or observation, and should demonstrate awareness of context and scope. We encourage writing that is precise, reflective, and attentive to its own limits.

Research Essays

Research essays should present a sustained and original argument. They may draw on theoretical, empirical, or archival work, but must demonstrate conceptual clarity, methodological coherence, and a clear line of thought. Submissions should also reflect on how their knowledge is produced and situated.

Nazar (Photo Essays)

Nazar is a quarterly photo essay series organised around a shared theme. Each contributor may submit up to three images. Each image must be accompanied by a caption (100–150 words), along with a short note (300–500 words) that situates the work within the theme.

We are not looking for purely aesthetic images. Submissions should reflect a considered way of seeing — attentive to context, framing, and perspective. What matters is not only what is shown, but how it is seen and from where.

Keyword Digest

Keyword entries engage with terms that shape how we think across disciplines. Rather than offering fixed definitions, submissions should trace how a term is used, contested, and reworked across contexts. Entries should attend to the histories and tensions of the term, while remaining accessible to a broad readership.

What We Do Not Publish

  • Academic papers written exclusively for specialist journals
  • Fiction or poetry (unless explicitly invited)
  • Submissions that are purely descriptive or lack a clear line of argument
  • Opinion pieces that are ungrounded or rhetorical
  • Work that speaks on behalf of marginalised communities without adequate context or engagement

What Makes a Submission Publishable

A submission is considered for publication if it demonstrates:

  • A clear and coherent line of thought
  • Engagement with context, whether through reading, observation, or experience
  • Awareness of how its claims are being made
  • Attention to complexity without unnecessary abstraction
  • An ability to open up a question, rather than simply assert a position

We are less interested in definitive answers and more in work that sustains inquiry.

Submission Process

Submissions must be made through info@thekural.in. Contributors are required to select the relevant category while submitting.

Receipt of submission will be acknowledged within one week. Please note that acknowledgment does not imply acceptance. All submissions are reviewed by the editorial team. Selected pieces may undergo revisions as part of the editorial process.

Timeline

  • Initial response: within one week
  • Editorial and review process: typically 4–6 weeks

Time-sensitive submissions may be reviewed on a shorter timeline.

Editorial Note

We encourage contributors to write from their own location and standpoint. Submissions engaging with marginalised experiences should do so with care, attention to voice, and awareness of representation. We value writing that is attentive to whose knowledge is being produced, and how.

Originality and Use of AI

Submissions must be original and authored by the contributor. Plagiarism will result in rejection. Limited use of AI tools (for proofreading or minor editing) is acceptable, but must be disclosed at the time of submission. Fully AI-generated content will not be considered.

Republication Policy

Authors may republish their work elsewhere with the acknowledgment: "This article was first published in [Platform Name], Date." Third-party reproduction requires prior permission from both the author(s) and the platform.
